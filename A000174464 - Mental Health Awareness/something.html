The Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH)

Background/Purpose of the Brief: Our team has chosen The Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH) charity as our philanthropic area because we believe there is an urgent need to improve care and services provided to those living with mental health issues across Canada. We have identified a gap within this sector that needs addressing and we believe that with innovative thinking and creative solutions, this gap can be filled.

Overall Charitable Landscape: According to the Canada Revenue Agency (2020), Canadians gave $11.19 billion in donations in 2018, a 5% decrease from 2017. The largest amount of donations were given to “humanitarian aid” causes at 32%, followed by health care/hospitals at 21%. Mental health charities such as CAMH only accounted for 4% of total donations made in 2018.

Competitive Considerations: The Creative Challenge:

The problem we are trying to solve is the lack of support and resources available for those living with mental health issues across Canada. We believe that there is a need for greater awareness of this issue and more funding from both government and private sources in order to provide better access to care and services. We also believe that innovative thinking and creative solutions can help fill this gap by engaging the public in conversations about mental health, increasing donations to organizations such as CAMH, and improving treatment options. The value that would be created by doing something new is increased awareness and understanding of mental health, which in turn leads to greater acceptance and support for those living with it. Furthermore, the implementation of innovative solutions could lead to more resources being allocated to this cause and improved access to care for those who need it most.

Summary of Recommendations:

Our team has identified five creative recommendations that we believe will help address the gap within the charitable/NFP arena related to mental health:
  • Develop a comprehensive public education campaign aimed at increasing awareness about mental health issues and reducing stigma surrounding them. This can include both traditional methods such as television commercials as well as digital platforms such as social media campaigns;
  • Establish a grant program for mental health research initiatives that will help advance knowledge and promote understanding about this issue;
  • Increase funding from both government and private sources in order to provide better access to care and services for those living with mental illness;
  • Create an online platform that allows potential donors to easily search for charities related to mental health and make donations quickly and securely;
    • Develop partnerships with other organizations such as universities, hospitals, etc., in order to increase awareness of the issue.

The net impression we want to achieve after launching our recommendations is increased support for those living with mental health issues. We want people to be more aware of the available resources, treatment options, etc., so they can get the help they need. We also want to create a more accepting and understanding environment for those living with mental illness so that they can live their lives without fear of judgement or discrimination.
