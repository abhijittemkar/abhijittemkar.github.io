Awareness on mental illness and discrimination

  • Abhijit Temkar

    • Background

    Our team has chosen The Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH) charity as our philanthropic area because we believe there is an urgent need to improve care and services provided to those living with mental health issues across Canada. We have identified a gap within this sector that needs addressing and we believe that with innovative thinking and creative solutions, this gap can be filled. Mental health issues are often stigmatized and discriminated against in society. People living with mental illness are often perceived as being “less than” by those who do not have the same experiences, which can lead to further marginalization of these individuals (CAMH, 2019). This discrimination can be seen in a variety of forms such as workplace discrimination and unequal access to healthcare services (CAMH, 2019).

    Overall Charitable Landscape



    According to the most recent data available from Statistics Canada, Canadians donated a total of $10.4 billion to charitable and non-profit organizations in 2018 (Statistics Canada, 2020). This represents a slight increase from the previous year's donation total of $10.2 billion.

    Religious organizations received the largest share of charitable donations in 2018, accounting for 36% of all donations. Other popular causes included social services (16%), health organizations (11%), and education and research organizations (10%) (Statistics Canada, 2020).

    Over the past five years, donation levels in Canada have remained relatively stable, with only small fluctuations from year to year. However, the COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on charitable giving, with many organizations reporting decreases in donations due to economic uncertainty and other factors (Imagine Canada, 2020).

    Despite this, there has been a surge in charitable giving towards causes related to social justice and racial equity in the wake of the Black Lives Matter protests and other events highlighting systemic inequalities in Canada and around the world (CBC News, 2020). It remains to be seen whether this trend will continue in the coming years.



    Social and demographic trends in Canada have had a significant impact on the charitable market in recent years. One notable trend is the growing interest in charitable causes related to social justice and equity, including issues such as homelessness, poverty, and racial inequality. This trend is likely a reflection of broader social and political changes, including the increasing recognition of systemic injustices and the need for action to address these issues.

    Some causes that are currently overrepresented in the Canadian charitable market include health organizations, religious organizations, and social services. These causes often have large established networks and receive significant support from government funding and other sources. However, there are also many important causes that are currently underrepresented in the charitable market, such as mental health, environmental conservation, and animal welfare.

    There are also emerging causes that are in need of social support, such as initiatives focused on supporting LGBTQ+ communities, advancing Indigenous rights, and promoting gender equality. These causes are often led by grassroots organizations and may not yet have the same level of visibility or support as more established causes.

    In terms of donation trends amongst Canadians, there are significant differences based on demographic variables such as age, gender, and occupation. For example, younger Canadians tend to be more interested in social justice causes and may be more likely to give to online crowdfunding campaigns or social media campaigns. Women are more likely than men to donate to charities and are more likely to support causes related to health and education. Meanwhile, those with higher incomes and education levels are more likely to donate to arts and culture organizations.

    Overall, Canadians tend to have a positive view of giving to charities, with surveys indicating that the majority of Canadians view charitable giving as an important part of their personal values and beliefs (Imagine Canada, 2020). However, there is also some skepticism around the effectiveness and transparency of charities, with many Canadians expressing concern about where their donations are going and how they are being used. This has led to a growing emphasis on transparency and accountability in the charitable sector, as well as increased scrutiny of charitable organizations by both the public and regulatory bodies.



    Technology has had a significant impact on how Canadians donate to charities and non-profit organizations. Online giving platforms have made it easier and more convenient for Canadians to donate to causes they care about, regardless of their location or schedule. In addition, social media has become an increasingly popular way for Canadians to learn about and engage with charitable causes, with many organizations using social media platforms to promote their campaigns and connect with potential donors.

    Mobile giving has also become more prevalent in recent years, with many Canadians using their smartphones and other mobile devices to donate to charities on-the-go. This has made it easier for Canadians to support causes they care about in real-time, such as in response to natural disasters or other emergencies.

    Furthermore, technology has enabled new forms of giving, such as crowdfunding and peer-to-peer fundraising. These approaches allow individuals and organizations to leverage their personal networks and social media followings to raise money for specific causes or projects. Crowdfunding campaigns have become particularly popular among younger Canadians, who may be more comfortable using online platforms to donate and engage with charitable causes.

    Overall, technology has made charitable giving more accessible, convenient, and interactive for Canadians. It has also opened up new avenues for engagement and collaboration between charities and their supporters, enabling more creative and effective approaches to fundraising and advocacy. As technology continues to evolve, it is likely that we will see even more innovation in the charitable sector and new ways for Canadians to support the causes they care about.



    Through this analysis, several observations can be made that have implications for the philanthropic area of mental health and addiction support, which is the focus of our team's chosen charity, The Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH).

    Firstly, there is a growing recognition of the importance of mental health and social justice causes in the charitable market, which may present opportunities for CAMH to increase its visibility and engagement with potential donors. However, it is important for CAMH to be proactive in communicating its mission and impact to potential donors, and to develop creative and innovative approaches to fundraising and advocacy that resonate with these causes.

    Secondly, technology has had a significant impact on how Canadians donate to charities, with online giving platforms and social media playing an increasingly important role. CAMH could benefit from investing in its online presence and developing a strong social media strategy to engage with potential donors, particularly younger Canadians who may be more comfortable with these technologies.

    Thirdly, there is a growing emphasis on transparency and accountability in the charitable sector, which may require CAMH to be more transparent about its fundraising practices and financial management. This could involve providing more detailed information about how donations are used and the impact they have, as well as implementing rigorous reporting and evaluation processes to ensure that donor funds are used effectively.

    Overall, these observations suggest that there are both opportunities and challenges facing CAMH and the broader mental health and addiction support sector in Canada. By staying attuned to these trends and being proactive in adapting to changes in the philanthropic landscape, CAMH can continue to advance its mission of improving care and services provided to those living with mental health issues across Canada

    Charity (NFP) Analysis



    Our team has chosen The Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH) charity as our philanthropic area. CAMH is Canada's largest mental health and addiction teaching hospital, as well as one of the world's leading research centres in the field of mental health. CAMH provides a wide range of services and programs, including clinical care, research, education, and advocacy, with the goal of improving the lives of people affected by mental illness and addiction.

    The gap we are trying to fill is the lack of access to high-quality mental health care and support services for individuals and communities across Canada. Mental health issues continue to be stigmatized and discriminated against in society, which can lead to further marginalization of those who are living with these conditions. As a result, many Canadians do not have access to the care and support they need to manage their mental health and achieve their full potential.

    Our team believes that CAMH is uniquely positioned to address this gap through its comprehensive range of programs and services, as well as its commitment to advancing knowledge and understanding of mental health and addiction through research and education. By supporting CAMH, we hope to contribute to a more equitable and accessible mental health care system in Canada, and to improve the lives of individuals and communities affected by mental illness and addiction.



    According to CAMH's annual reports, the charity has seen a steady increase in donations over the past five years. In 2016-2017, CAMH received $27.3 million in donations, while in 2020-2021, it received $45.2 million, representing a significant increase in giving over this period.

    This growth in donations can be attributed to a number of factors, including increased awareness of mental health issues and the importance of supporting organizations like CAMH that provide critical care and services in this area. Additionally, CAMH has been proactive in developing innovative fundraising strategies, such as its One Brave Night campaign, which encourages Canadians to raise funds and awareness for mental health by staying up all night.

    Overall, the donation trends to CAMH over the past five years suggest that there is growing attention and support for mental health causes in Canada, and that CAMH is well-positioned to capitalize on this trend through its comprehensive range of programs and services.

    The Situational Analysis

    In the past, mental health issues were not widely recognized as a major health concern in Canada, and as a result, mental health care and support services were often underfunded and under-resourced. This lack of attention and investment had significant consequences for individuals and communities affected by mental illness, including limited access to care, high rates of stigma and discrimination, and poor health outcomes.

    In recent years, there has been a growing recognition of the importance of mental health in Canada, and organizations like CAMH have played an important role in raising awareness and advocating for better mental health care and support. However, there is still significant baggage in the form of stigma and discrimination, which can hinder efforts to address the mental health crisis and create lasting change.

    Currently, CAMH is one of the leading organizations working to improve mental health care and support in Canada. CAMH provides a wide range of services, including clinical care, research, education, and advocacy, and is committed to advancing knowledge and understanding of mental health and addiction. However, despite these efforts, there are still significant gaps in access to care and support, particularly for marginalized communities and those living in remote or underserved areas.

    There are also challenges in terms of funding and resource allocation for mental health care and support, which can limit the ability of organizations like CAMH to provide the level of care and support that is needed. In addition, there is still significant stigma and discrimination around mental health issues in Canada, which can prevent individuals from seeking care and support and contribute to poor health outcomes.

    The ideal solution for mental health care and support in Canada is one that is comprehensive, accessible, and equitable for all individuals and communities. This solution should prioritize early intervention and prevention, as well as provide a range of evidence-based treatments and supports that are tailored to individual needs.

    If we get this right, the world will be a better place for everyone. Individuals and communities affected by mental illness will have access to the care and support they need to manage their conditions and achieve their full potential. This, in turn, will lead to improved health outcomes, increased productivity, and greater social and economic well-being for all Canadians. Additionally, by reducing stigma and discrimination around mental health issues, we can create a more compassionate and inclusive society that values the well-being of all its members.

    1. Funding and resource allocation: One of the biggest roadblocks to improving mental health care and support is a lack of funding and resources. Mental health care and support services require significant investment, and there is often competition for limited resources within the healthcare system.
    2. Stigma and discrimination: Stigma and discrimination around mental health issues remain a significant barrier to care and support. Despite efforts to reduce stigma and increase awareness, there is still a lack of understanding and acceptance of mental health issues in many parts of Canadian society.
    3. Accessibility: Many individuals and communities in Canada face significant barriers to accessing mental health care and support, including geography, income, and cultural and linguistic differences.
    4. Workforce shortages: There is a shortage of mental health professionals in many parts of Canada, which can limit access to care and support.
    5. Resistance to change: Finally, there may be resistance to change from some stakeholders, including healthcare providers, policymakers, and members of the public who may not recognize the importance of mental health care and support.

    Our team is concerned about these potential roadblocks and fears that they could hinder efforts to improve mental health care and support in Canada. We recognize that addressing these obstacles will require a collaborative and multi-faceted approach that involves stakeholders from across the healthcare system and society as a whole. We are committed to working creatively and innovatively to overcome these challenges and make progress towards our goal of improving mental health care and support for all Canadians.

    The target audience for our idea and improvement is anyone in Canada who is affected by mental health issues, whether directly or indirectly. This includes individuals living with mental health issues, their families and caregivers, mental health professionals, policymakers, and the wider public.

    We need to convince several stakeholders that this innovation needs to take place. These include policymakers at the federal, provincial, and municipal levels, healthcare providers and administrators, mental health advocacy groups, and members of the public. We need to make the case that improving mental health care and support is not only a moral imperative but also an economic and social necessity.

    Once implemented, this idea will impact a wide range of individuals and communities in Canada. Direct beneficiaries will include individuals living with mental health issues and their families and caregivers, who will have access to better and more comprehensive care and support. Mental health professionals will also benefit from improved resources and support. Indirect beneficiaries will include members of the wider public, who will benefit from increased awareness and understanding of mental health issues, as well as reduced social and economic costs associated with mental health problems. Ultimately, the entire Canadian society will benefit from a healthier, more productive, and more resilient population.

    Competitive Considerations

    1. Bell Let's Talk: This is a mental health initiative by Bell Canada aimed at raising awareness and funds for mental health support across Canada. They have a strong presence in the media and use social media to engage with donors and raise funds.
    2. The Mental Health Commission of Canada: This organization works to improve mental health services and promote mental health across Canada. They collaborate with governments, organizations, and individuals to create a coordinated approach to mental health care and support.
    3. The Canadian Mental Health Association: This is a national organization that works to promote the mental health of all Canadians and improve access to mental health services. They provide advocacy, education, and support for individuals and families affected by mental health issues.

    These organizations have strong brand recognition and established fundraising initiatives, which may make it challenging for new organizations to gain attention and funding in the marketplace. However, there is a growing awareness and recognition of the need to improve mental health care and support in Canada, which presents an opportunity for new ideas and innovations to make an impact. Collaborating with these organizations may also provide opportunities to leverage their resources and expertise to create a more comprehensive and coordinated approach to mental health care and support.

    1. Strong brand recognition: These organizations have established themselves as leaders in the mental health field, with recognizable logos and branding that help them stand out in the marketplace.
    2. Media presence: Many of these organizations have used traditional and social media to raise awareness about mental health issues and their initiatives. This includes campaigns and events that receive widespread coverage, helping to generate interest and support from donors.
    3. Fundraising events: These organizations host fundraising events, such as walks or runs, that help engage donors and raise funds for mental health support.
    4. Partnerships and collaborations: By partnering with other organizations, these mental health charities are able to amplify their message and reach a wider audience of potential donors. This may include collaborations with celebrities or other high-profile individuals who can help bring attention to their cause.

    As a result of these strategies, these organizations have been successful in attracting the attention of donors and raising significant funds for mental health support. However, there is still a need for more resources and support in this area, which presents an opportunity for new ideas and approaches to make an impact.
